Ubiquitous Computing: Haptic Vest for NASA

Time Period: Spring 2015

I worked with 3 other students to develop a prototype to help NASA develop tools for astronauts. NASA requested a wearable device to help astronauts navigate on planetary surfaces where they might not have audio or visual contact. We developed a prototype of a vest with vibration motors to provide haptic feedback. At the end of the semester, we presented at NASA's JSC Wearable Technology Symposium in Houston, TX.

Service Design: Food in English Avenue

Time Period: Spring 2014

Taking a graduate-level studio course to fulfill an undergraduate requirement, I worked with a team in a local Atlanta neighborhood to reimagine ways to obtain food and eat healthy. We did initial research, held workshops with neighborhood members using service design activities we created, synthesized our results, and came up with documentation of our ideas for next steps. Instead of creating a hastily-made product or system, we developed resources for future use by the community.
