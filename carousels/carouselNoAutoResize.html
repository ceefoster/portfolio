Our final product. Important design decisions are the main right control panel, with the "How to use this application" feature and labels for each slider.
To start, I sketched various possibilities before my team member worked on the code. We started with labels for each slider and discussed vocabulary like "more" and "higher".
We decided to have “Low is important” and “High is important” labels only at the top to avoid clutter. I thought of having a checkbox to make the variable active (in the model) or inactive (removed from the model).
We presented this to our partners. They were confused about the sliders, and didn't relate the labels at the top to all of the sliders. We went back to the drawing table and reevaluated.
An example of our final slider, one of three. We added descriptive labels to each slider based on feedback. Our partners also said that once someone knew how to use it, they wouldn't want to see the "How to" text everytime. We incorporated this into a drop-down menu link, as shown on the first slide in the final version.
